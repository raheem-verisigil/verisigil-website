Generate an EU AI Act Compliance Report
in 5 Minutes

By Raheem Larry Babatunde · VeriSigil AI · May 7, 2026 · 4 minutes
⏱ This guide takes exactly 5 minutes. No signup required. At the end you will have a verifiable compliance record for your AI agent.

The EU AI Act requires documented compliance evidence for high-risk AI agents. This is not just a policy document — regulators can ask for technical proof at any time after August 1, 2026. Here is the fastest way to generate that evidence.

Step 1 — Run the Free Security Scanner (60 seconds)

Go to verisigilai.com/scanner.html. Paste your agent configuration — JSON, YAML, or plain text — and click Scan. You will get:

  • Risk score 0–100
  • EU AI Act risk classification (minimal / limited / high / unacceptable)
  • 8-point security check with findings
  • Shareable report URL

This scan takes 3 seconds and requires no account.

Step 2 — Issue a Cryptographic Passport (2 minutes)

Go to verisigilai.com/quickstart.html and use the sandbox key to issue a passport for your agent:

  • Your agent gets a W3C DID — a cryptographically verifiable identity
  • EU AI Act risk class is embedded in the passport at issuance
  • An immutable audit trail begins from the moment of issuance
  • Every verification is logged with a cryptographic signature

The entire process takes one API call. The sandbox key is: verisigil-secret-2026

Step 3 — Get the Public Verification URL (30 seconds)

After issuing the passport, your agent gets a public report page at:

verisigilai.com/agent.html?id=vsa_YOUR_AGENT_ID

This URL shows the agent's trust score, EU risk class, issuer, verification history, and Shadow Detection status. It is publicly accessible and cryptographically verifiable. This is the URL you share with auditors.

Step 4 — Add the Trust Badge to Your Documentation (30 seconds)

Go to verisigilai.com/badge.html and generate an embeddable trust badge. Add it to your API documentation, README, or product website:

  • HTML embed — one line of code
  • Markdown — one line for your README
  • React component — for your product dashboard

What You Now Have

After these 4 steps, you have:

  • A cryptographic agent identity (W3C DID, Ed25519 signed)
  • An EU AI Act risk classification embedded in the passport
  • An immutable audit trail from date of issuance
  • A publicly verifiable compliance URL for auditors
  • An embeddable trust badge for your product

This satisfies the identity, transparency, and traceability requirements of the EU AI Act for limited-risk agents. High-risk agents have additional requirements — see our full checklist.
